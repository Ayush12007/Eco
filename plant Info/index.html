Lotus

  • Lotus plants are aquatic and thrive in water.
  • They prefer full sun, meaning they need at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
  • Lotus plants grow best in loamy soil that is rich in organic matter.
  • Lotus plants prefer warm temperatures .

Tulip

  • Tulips generally require regular watering, especially during their active growth phase in spring.
  • They need at least 6 hours of direct sunlight per day to bloom properly.
  • Sandy or loamy soil that is rich in organic matter is ideal for tulips.
  • Tulips like soil that is consistently moist but not waterlogged.

Sunflower

  • Provide them with about 1 to 1.5 inches of water per week.
  • Plant them in an area that receives at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight per day for optimal growth and flowering.
  • Sunflowers prefer well-draining soil with a pH level between 6.0 and 7.5.
  • They also benefit from soil that is well-drained and not waterlogged.

Rose

  • Roses generally require about 1 inch of water per week.
  • Roses thrive in full sunlight, meaning they need at least 6 hours of direct sunlight per day.
  • Well-draining soil is crucial for roses. Sandy loam or loamy soil is ideal.
  • Mulching around the base of the plants helps retain moisture and suppress weeds.

Aloe Vera

  • Allow soil to dry out completely between waterings.
  • Prefers bright, indirect light but can also tolerate partial shade.
  • Well-draining soil mix, such as cactus or succulent potting mix.
  • Aloe vera prefers low to moderate humidity levels.

Amaryllis

  • Keep soil evenly moist during active gyrowth, but allow it to dry slightly between waterings.
  • Requires bright, indirect sunlight.
  • Well-draining potting mix, such as a blend of potting soil and perlite.
  • Ensure proper drainage to prevent waterlogging, especially during dormancy.

Cactus

  • Water sparingly, allowing the soil to dry out completely between waterings.
  • Requires plenty of direct sunlight, at least 6 hours per day.
  • Well-draining soil mix specifically formulated for cacti and succulents.
  • Cacti prefer dry conditions and are adapted to arid environments, so avoid excessive moisture in the soil.

Apple Plant

  • Regular watering, especially during the growing season.
  • Full sun, requiring at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily for optimal growth and fruit production.
  • Sandy loam or loamy soil rich in organic matter is ideal.
  • Adequate moisture is essential, but avoid waterlogged conditions.

Azalea

  • Regular watering, keeping the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
  • Partial shade to filtered sunlight, especially during the hottest part of the day.
  • Well-draining, acidic soil with a pH between 4.5 and 6.0.
  • Azaleas prefer moist conditions but should not be waterlogged.

Baby's Breath

  • Infrequent but deep watering once established, allowing the soil to dry slightly between waterings.
  • Full sun to partial shade, especially in hot climates where some afternoon shade may be beneficial.
  • Well-draining soil with a slightly alkaline pH. Sandy soil amended with organic matter is ideal.
  • Prefers slightly dry to moderately moist conditions once established.

Bamboo

  • Regular watering, keeping the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
  • Prefers partial shade to full sun, depending on the species. Some varieties can tolerate full sun.
  • Well-draining, fertile soil with good organic content. A slightly acidic to neutral pH is preferred.
  • Requires consistently moist soil, especially during the growing season.

Begonia

  • Keep soil evenly moist but not waterlogged. Allow the top inch of soil to dry out between waterings.
  • Prefers bright, indirect light or partial shade. Avoid direct sunlight.
  • Well-draining potting mix with good aeration.
  • Requires moderate to high humidity levels. Use a pebble tray or room humidifier to increase humidity around the plant.

Black-eyed Susan

  • Requires moderate watering, allowing the soil to dry slightly between waterings.
  • Requires at least 6 hours of sunlight daily for optimal growth and flowering.
  • Amend heavy clay soil with organic matter to improve drainage.
  • Prefers average moisture levels. Avoid waterlogged soil.

Bleeding Heart

  • Keep the soil consistently moist, especially during periods of active growth and flowering.
  • Prefers partial to full shade, especially in hotter climates.
  • Well-draining, humus-rich soil that retains moisture but does not become waterlogged.
  • Mulching can help retain soil moisture and regulate temperature fluctuations.

Bonsai Tree

  • Water thoroughly when the soil surface starts to dry out, but avoid waterlogging.
  • Most bonsai trees prefer bright, indirect sunlight.
  • Well-draining soil mix specifically formulated for bonsai trees.
  • Maintain moderate humidity levels around the bonsai tree, especially indoors.

Buttercup

  • Well-draining, fertile soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH.
  • Maintain moderate to high moisture levels in the soil, especially during hot weather.

Coneflower

  • Prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.
  • Requires full sun to thrive, at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight per day.
  • Thrives in average to poor soil conditions, as long as it's well-draining.
  • Avoid overwatering, as it can lead to root rot. Once established, coneflowers are fairly low-maintenance.

Cosmos

  • Requires moderate watering, keeping the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
  • Thrives in full sun, requiring at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight per day.
  • Prefers well-drained, moderately fertile soil with a pH around 6.0 to 7.0.
  • Avoid overwatering, as it can cause root rot. Once established, cosmos are somewhat drought-tolerant

Crocus

  • Requires moderate watering during the growing season.
  • Prefers full sun to partial shade. Requires at least 6 hours of direct sunlight per day for optimal growth and flowering.
  • Thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soil with good organic content.
  • Avoid overwatering, especially during dormancy, as it can cause the bulbs to rot.

Cyclamen

  • Prefers bright, indirect light. Avoid exposing it to direct sunlight.
  • Needs well-draining soil rich in organic matter. A slightly acidic to neutral pH (around 6.0 to 7.0) is ideal.
  • Keep humidity levels high around the plant.

Daffodil

  • Daffodils prefer regular watering, especially during their active growth period in spring.
  • Thrives in full sun to partial shade. Ideally, they should receive at least 6 hours of sunlight per day.
  • Requires well-draining soil with good organic content.
  • Avoid waterlogged conditions, as excessive moisture can lead to bulb rot.

Grapes Plant

  • Thrives in full sun, requiring at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight daily for optimal growth .
  • Requires well-draining soil with good fertility and a pH level between 5.5 and 7.0.
  • While they need consistent moisture, avoid overwatering, as it can lead to root rot.

Dandelion

  • Dandelions are drought-tolerant and don't require much watering.

Dogwood

  • Dogwoods prefer acidic, organic-rich soil. Amending the soil with compost before planting can improve soil structure and fertility.
  • Dogwoods prefer moderate moisture levels.
